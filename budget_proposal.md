# Ogaloader Budget Proposal (18-Month Runway) - Nigerian Context

## 1. Executive Summary
This budget is grounded in the current economic realities of Nigeria. It proposes an 18-month runway costed in Nigerian Naira (₦), focusing on the capital needed to achieve Product-Market Fit in a challenging but highly rewarding logistics environment.

This model factors in the realities of acquiring local tech talent, high localized operational costs (such as 24/7 reliable power for dispatch centers), FX volatility for cloud services, and the crucial necessity of "boots-on-the-ground" marketing at physical truck parks (e.g., Apapa, Tin Can) and open markets (e.g., Alaba, Mile 12). 

*Total Seed Funding Goal: ₦131,000,000 – ₦214,000,000 (Approx. $85k - $140k USD equivalent).*

## 2. Capital Expenditure (CapEx): Pre-Launch Setup (Months 1–6)

### 2.1 Technology & Product Development
Building the MVP requires a dedicated, in-house or closely managed local tech team.
*   **Engineering Team:** 3 mid-to-senior devs (Node.js/React/Mobile) for 6 months. Retaining good talent in Lagos requires competitive Naira salaries.
*   **UI/UX Design:** Freelance or contract designer for localizing the user flows.
*   **Cloud Infrastructure & APIs (FX Exposed):** AWS/GCP hosting, Google Maps API (critical for tracking), and Termii/Twilio (for SMS OTPs). These are billed in USD, so buffer is added for FX fluctuations.
*   *Estimated Subtotal: ₦22,000,000 - ₦37,000,000*

### 2.2 Legal, Compliance & Physical Setup
*   **Corporate Governance:** CAC Registration, Trademarking, and drawing up solid Terms of Service mapped to Nigerian law.
*   **Compliance & Licenses:** NDPR data compliance and establishing Goods-in-Transit (GIT) insurance framework with local insurers (Leadway, AIICO).
*   **Command Center Setup:** Office lease (1-2 years in a central tech hub like Yaba or Ikeja), laptops, 24/7 internet, and critically: **Inverters, Solar, or heavy Generator setup for uninterrupted power**.
*   *Estimated Subtotal: ₦8,000,000 - ₦17,000,000*

## 3. Operating Expenditure (OpEx): Post-Launch (Months 7–18)

### 3.1 Payroll & Core Operations
*   **Founders & Core Team:** Lean stipends for founders, plus competitive salaries for an Operations Manager and Customer Success lead.
*   **Dispatch Command Center:** 24/7 rotational staff to monitor trips and handle emergencies.
*   **Field Agents & Union Liaisons:** Highly essential in the Nigerian system. Salaries and fuel/transport stipends for agents constantly visiting truck parks to onboard drivers and physical markets to verify sellers.
*   **Office Maintenance:** Monthly generator fueling, broadband subscriptions, and general admin.
*   *Estimated Subtotal (Over 12 months): ₦61,000,000 - ₦95,000,000*

### 3.2 Marketing & Liquidity Incentives
*   **Digital & Traditional Ads:** Targeted social media ads for agricultural and construction businesses, plus localized radio jingles (often highly effective for truck drivers).
*   **"First Trip" Incentives:** Heavily subsidizing early transactions (e.g., giving drivers a ₦10,000 bonus on their first completed trip, or waiving logistics fees for a buyer's first bulk order) to artificially jumpstart platform liquidity.
*   **Physical Branding (Crucial):** High-visibility Ogaloader reflective vests for drivers, branded truck decals, and official IDs to build immediate physical trust in notoriously skeptical local markets.
*   *Estimated Subtotal: ₦28,000,000 - ₦45,000,000*
